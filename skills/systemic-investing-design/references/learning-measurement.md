# Layer 5 — Learning, Measurement & Adaptation

Sources: Michael Quinn Patton (*Developmental Evaluation*, 2011; *Principles-Focused Evaluation*, 2018; *Blue Marble Evaluation*, 2019, and the Blue Marble network), TransCap (systemic intelligence, system-health measurement), Tamarack (per-archetype evaluation questions; the warning against standardized metrics across mixed portfolios), Preskill & Beer (*Evaluating Social Innovation*).

Core idea: a systemic portfolio judged by project-grant logic (predefined KPIs, attribution, end-of-causal-chain outputs) is structurally guaranteed to look like a failure — or worse, to succeed by quietly becoming a deal pipeline. Layer 5 exists to give the strategy an evaluation paradigm that matches its epistemology, and to make learning a governed, funded function rather than a reporting chore.

## Developmental evaluation (the operating paradigm)

Neither formative ("improve the program") nor summative ("judge the program") but **developmental**: evaluation embedded in the strategy team as a real-time navigation instrument under complexity.

Practical commitments:
- **The evaluator is inside the work** — a critical friend on the strategy council, present at sensemaking, not an external auditor arriving at year three.
- **Track emergence, not fidelity.** The question is "what is the system doing in response, and what should we adapt?" — not "did we execute the plan?" Document pivots and the evidence behind them; in DE, a well-evidenced pivot *is* a success event.
- **Contribution over attribution.** Build plausible contribution narratives (timeline + mechanism + corroborating actors) instead of chasing counterfactual attribution that complexity won't yield. Brief boards on this distinction before deployment, not after the first awkward review.
- **Principles-focused when outcomes are distant**: evaluate whether action is consistent with the strategy's principles (e.g., "community decision power", "catalytic positions must crowd in") — principles are evaluable years before system outcomes are visible (GUIDE test: a good principle is Guiding, Useful, Inspiring, Developmental, Evaluable).

## Blue Marble: evaluate transformation by transformation criteria

When the intent is transformation, the evaluand is **the system and the portfolio's coherence**, not each initiative:

- **Transformation criteria, not project criteria** — is the system's structure changing (new feedback loops, shifted rules, changed resource flows, moved mental models)? Map indicators to the layer-2 loops and six conditions, not just to intervention outputs.
- **Transboundary thinking** — follow effects across the sector/nation/discipline silos the way the system does; watch for displacement (the "improvement" that exported the problem past the boundary).
- **Portfolio-level coherence as an evaluand** — are interventions actually interacting (Tamarack's Synergy questions: reinforcement visible? partners adapting to each other?), or is this a set of parallel projects?
- **Local-global nesting** — connect place-based signals to the larger systems the place sits in (a food-system portfolio reads policy, market, and climate signals beyond its boundary).

## A three-altitude indicator set

Resist the single-dashboard urge; hold three altitudes with different cadences:

| Altitude | What to watch | Example signals | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **System health** | The layer-2 loops and six conditions | New entrants; changed regulation; resource flows redirecting; narrative shift in local media; relationships forming across former silos | Annual deep read; ambient sensing |
| **Portfolio coherence** | Combinatorial effects; archetype-appropriate progress | Interventions referencing/using each other; co-investment crowding in; partners adapting plans; gaps closing (Strategic Gaps) or learning landing (Probes) | Quarterly sensemaking |
| **Intervention performance** | Conventional instrument-level metrics | Financial performance per instrument; milestone delivery; grantee health | Per instrument norms |

Use **Tamarack's per-archetype questions** to set expectations honestly: probes are evaluated on learning yield, not impact; Cabinets on insight and imagination surfaced; Synergy portfolios on interaction effects. Uniform KPIs across a mixed portfolio misrepresent everything they touch.

## The learning system (governed and funded)

- **Named owner** — a sensemaking lead (in the backbone if one exists) accountable for synthesis, with direct access to the strategy council.
- **Budget line** — typically 3–7% of deployment (layer 4); systemic intelligence is portfolio infrastructure, not overhead to minimize.
- **Cadence** — quarterly cross-portfolio sensemaking (investees + community + funders, pattern-spotting, not reporting); annual thesis review against the falsifiable statement from layer 3; pre-agreed **adaptation triggers** ("if X signal hasn't moved by date Y, we revisit sleeve Z") wired to the re-allocation rights in layer 4 governance.
- **Community as sense-makers, not data sources** — people living in the system interpret signals in the room, with the standing to change the strategy's mind.
- **Negative-results discipline** — publish or at least pool what didn't work (communities of practice like TWIST are the natural venue); the field's thin evidence base is itself a recognized barrier, and every strategy is a contributor to the commons.

## Anti-patterns

- **KPI colonization** — conventional metrics installed "just for the board pack" gradually re-selecting the portfolio toward the attributable. The coherence check runs both ways: layer 5 design protects layers 1–4.
- **Evaluation as year-3 procurement** — an external summative review bolted on after the fact; by then the learning moments are gone and the frame will be project logic.
- **Dashboard theater** — beautiful system dashboards nobody uses to decide anything. Every indicator should have a decision it could change; cut the rest.
- **Attribution chasing** — spending learning budget proving *we* caused it, at the expense of understanding *what is happening*.
- **Grading the wrong fish** — judging a probe portfolio on impact, a Cabinet on coherence, or a Synergy portfolio on deal-level returns (see the archetype table in `theory-of-transformation.md`).
